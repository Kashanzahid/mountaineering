ABOUT US


Mountain Gurus is a mountain guide service based in Washington State in the heart of the Cascade Mountain Range. We specialize in Seven Summits climbs, international mountaineering expeditions, and treks and adventures worldwide. We also offer a wide array of mountaineering courses and climbs throughout the Pacific Northwest.

Founded in 2008, we are a guide-owned and operated company. Our mountain guides are among the most dedicated in the industry and draw on extensive experience gained while exploring and climbing in some of the most remote places on earth. Our guides are highly skilled professionals committed to providing our clients with the individual care and safety they deserve.

At Mountain Gurus, we strive to provide each and every one of our customers with individual attention and a rewarding, personalized experience. As adventure travel experts, we will take the time to make sure all of your questions are answered. We provide the very best services and equipment to ensure that you enjoy a safe and successful journey while making memories to last a lifetime. Year after year, many of our clients return as friends to share in our passion for the mountains.

Our expeditions take place in some of the most stunning destinations on the planet, and we are dedicated to sharing these amazing places with you. Our programs are designed for climbers and outdoor enthusiasts of all skill levels. Whether you join our Intro to Mountaineering Course on the glaciers of Mount Baker, climb Mount Everest, or watch a pride of lions with us on the Serengeti, we will meet you at your current experience level and offer you an opportunity to safely explore beyond it. We believe that some of life’s richest experiences come from challenging yourself while exploring your world.

Our partner company, Northwest Alpine Guides, operates all of our alpine climbs, mountaineering schools and backcountry ski adventures in the Pacific Northwest. Offering ascents of iconic North American peaks such as Mount Rainier, Mount Baker and Mount Shuksan, our domestic itineraries are the perfect preparation for tackling the world’s largest mountains on one of our international expeditions.

Our Founder

Dennis Broadwell grew up on the south shore of Long Island far from any mountain top. As a teenager he discovered skiing in the Catskill Mountains and hiked his first peaks in the Adirondacks of New York. He quickly became an ADK 46er, ticking off the last of the 46 tallest peaks in the Adirondacks by his early twenties. He began guiding his friends up the peaks of New England, including Mount Washington in New Hampshire’s White Mountains, where he made numerous winter ascents. After graduating from college, he traveled extensively across America and overseas while continuing to pursue rock, ice and mountain climbing. Dennis conceived of the vision for Mountain Gurus in 1997, but it wasn’t until 2008 that he incorporated the company through the encouragement of his dear friend Kili Sherpa. Dennis’ passion for mountains and exploration has led him to climb and travel all over the world. He firmly believes:

“Challenging yourself while exploring your world is truly one of life’s greatest experiences”

During his decades of climbing and adventure travel, Dennis has seen time and time again that when we pursue our dreams through human-powered achievement in a community of fellow adventurers, we open ourselves to a world of rich experiences and an incredibly rewarding way to live. Or as George Mallory put it:

“If you cannot understand that there is something in man which responds to the challenge of this mountain and goes out to meet it, that the struggle is the struggle of
life itself upward and forever upward, then you won’t see why we go. What we get from this adventure is just sheer joy. And joy is, after all, the end of life. We do not
live to eat and make money. We eat and make money to be able to live. That is what life means and what life is for.”

Today, Mountain Gurus follows a philosophy rooted in guiding, instruction and training. Our guides and guests pursue shared goals together – pushing ourselves to defy gravity while climbing, trekking, skiing, developing relationships across cultures, and immersing ourselves in the natural world. We firmly believe in the transformative power of international journeys. After all, as Mark Twain wrote:

“Travel is fatal to prejudice, bigotry, and narrow-mindedness.”

It's a yak!

Is that a mountain goat, a buffalo, or a bison on the Mountain Gurus logo? If you are like us, you probably grew up in North America and have never encountered a yak.

The yak is a long-haired animal (Bovinae) found throughout the high mountains of the Himalayan region. Unlike a mountain goat, which is also an excellent climber that resides below 12,000 feet, the Yak's physiology is well adapted to much higher altitudes and lives only above 9,000 feet. Their larger lungs, weight bearing capacity, and perseverance in cold, harsh, and high elevations make the yak the ultimate mountain guru. The local people of the Himalaya and Tibetan Plateau depend on the yak for its abundant resources, plowing fields, hauling, dung for heating & fertilizer, milk, cheese, yogurt, natural fiber for clothing, bone, and even yak riding.

On many of our longer mountaineering expeditions, you will find yaks hard at work transporting food and heavy climbing gear to our base camps and to altitudes above 18,000 feet. The clang of yak bells can be heard throughout the high mountain trails of Nepal, Bhutan, India, Mongolia, and Tibet, serving as a constant reminder of what a truly special and enchanted habitat they live.
